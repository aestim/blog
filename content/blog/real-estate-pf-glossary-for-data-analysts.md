For a data analyst, the ability to influence business decisions starts with mastering the "language of the domain." In the world of **Real Estate Project Financing (PF)**, the data is only as good as the analyst's understanding of the underlying financial structures.

Using my current experience in the real estate development sector, combined with a technical background from the University of Edinburgh, I have compiled a glossary of essential terms. These are the building blocks I use to model risks for projects like the **Geomdan P2-Ju Parking Tower**.

------

### 1. Funding & Cost Structures

Understanding how a project is funded is the first step in identifying financial bottlenecks.

- **PF (Project Financing)**: A method of funding a project based on its projected cash flow rather than the developer's general balance sheet.
- **Equity**: The developer's own capital invested in the project. In our current modeling, identifying the impact of an **equity shortfall** is a critical stress test.
- **Bridge Loan**: A short-term, high-interest loan used to cover initial costs like land acquisition before permanent PF is secured.
- **All-in Cost**: The total effective interest rate, including the nominal rate and all upfront fees.
- **Spread**: The additional interest rate added to a base rate (like the CD 91-day rate) to reflect the specific risk profile of the project.

------

### 2. Valuation & Return Metrics

These metrics serve as the "North Star" for any data-driven valuation model.

- **NOI (Net Operating Income)**: Total annual income from rent and parking minus operating expenses, excluding taxes and interest.

- **Percentage Rent**: A lease structure where rent is calculated as a percentage of the tenant's gross sales, such as our **E-mart** contract.

- **Anchor Tenant**: A high-profile tenant (like E-mart) that ensures stable traffic and significantly reduces the risk profile of the asset.

------

### 3. Risk & Exit Strategy

These terms define the "breaking points" of a project's financial health.

- **LTV (Loan to Value)**: The ratio of the loan amount to the property's appraised value, which is critical for determining refinancing potential.
- **DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio)**: A measure of the project's ability to cover its debt payments using its net income.
- **Negative Carry**: A situation where interest costs exceed the income generated by the asset, a common risk before full occupancy.
- **Refinancing**: Replacing high-interest PF debt with lower-interest permanent loans once the project is stabilized, such as after the opening of the **Incheon District Court** in 2028.
- **EOD (Event of Default)**: A specific breach of contract that allows lenders to demand immediate repayment.

------

### Conclusion

Mastering these terms allows a data analyst to move beyond simple data processing and become a **strategic partner** in the decision-making process. By quantifying these variables, we can transform abstract risks into actionable insights.
